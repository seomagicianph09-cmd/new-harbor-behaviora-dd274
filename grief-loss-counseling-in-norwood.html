Frequently Asked Questions About Grief & Loss Counseling

What are the qualifications of the grief counselors at New Harbor Behavioral Healthcare in Norwood?
All of our grief and loss counselors are licensed clinical social workers, mental health counselors, or psychologists, each with specialized training in grief therapy. Our team members hold active Massachusetts licenses and participate in ongoing professional development focused on bereavement and trauma support.
How much does grief counseling typically cost, and do you accept insurance?
Our standard rate for individual grief counseling sessions ranges from $120 to $160 per 50-minute session. We are in-network with several major insurance providers in Massachusetts, including Blue Cross Blue Shield, Harvard Pilgrim, and Tufts. We also offer sliding scale options for Norwood-area residents who may not have insurance or have high out-of-pocket costs. Please contact our office to verify your specific coverage.
What is the typical process for starting grief counseling at your Norwood office?
The process begins with a brief phone consultation to discuss your needs and match you with an appropriate counselor. Your first session is an intake appointment, where you'll talk about your loss and set goals for therapy. Most clients attend weekly sessions, but we can adjust the frequency based on your preferences and needs. We provide both in-person sessions at our Norwood location and secure telehealth options for local residents.
How long does grief counseling usually last, and how will I know if it’s helping?
The duration of grief counseling varies—some clients find support in just a few sessions, while others may attend for several months. Progress is discussed regularly with your counselor, who will help you set personal milestones. Our goal is to help you feel supported and equipped with coping strategies, not to keep you in therapy longer than needed.
Do you guarantee results or provide any follow-up support after counseling ends?
While we cannot guarantee specific outcomes, we are committed to providing compassionate, evidence-based care tailored to each individual. After your sessions end, we offer check-in appointments and can connect you with local Norwood-area resources or support groups if you need ongoing support. Your counselor will work with you to create a plan for maintaining progress as you move forward.
