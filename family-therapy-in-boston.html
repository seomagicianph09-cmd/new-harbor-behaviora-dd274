Frequently Asked Questions About Family Therapy

What are the typical costs for family therapy sessions at New Harbor Behavioral Healthcare in Boston?
At New Harbor Behavioral Healthcare, family therapy sessions typically range from $150 to $225 per 50-minute session, depending on the therapist’s experience and the specific needs of your family. We accept most major insurance plans in Massachusetts, and our admin team is happy to help you verify your coverage before your first visit. Sliding scale options may be available for families facing financial hardship. Please contact our office for an individualized estimate.
How long does the family therapy process usually take before we see results?
The duration of family therapy varies by family, but many of our Boston-area clients begin to see noticeable improvements within 6 to 12 sessions, typically scheduled weekly or biweekly. For some families, addressing specific communication or conflict issues may take fewer sessions, while more complex challenges can require a longer-term approach. Your therapist will work with you to set clear goals and regularly review progress to ensure the process remains tailored to your needs.
Are your therapists licensed and familiar with issues specific to families in the Boston area?
Yes, all therapists at New Harbor Behavioral Healthcare are fully licensed in Massachusetts as LMFTs (Licensed Marriage and Family Therapists), LICSWs (Licensed Independent Clinical Social Workers), or Psychologists. Many have advanced training in areas relevant to Boston families, such as navigating school systems, multicultural dynamics, and stressors unique to urban living. Our team regularly participates in continuing education to stay current with best practices.
What should our family expect during the first therapy session?
During your first session at our Boston office, your therapist will meet with your family to discuss your concerns, gather background information, and understand each member’s perspective. The initial session is a time for everyone to express their hopes and goals for therapy. Your therapist will explain confidentiality, office policies, and answer any questions. By the end of the session, you’ll collaborate on a plan for moving forward, including frequency of sessions and initial focus areas.
Do you offer any guarantees or follow-up support after therapy ends?
While we cannot guarantee specific outcomes due to the personal nature of therapy, New Harbor Behavioral Healthcare is committed to supporting your family’s progress. We provide detailed feedback and practical tools to use outside of sessions. After completing therapy, families are welcome to schedule periodic check-ins or booster sessions as needed. If new challenges arise, we’re here to help you navigate them with ongoing support tailored to your evolving needs.
