Frequently Asked Questions About Telehealth Counseling

Are your therapists licensed to provide telehealth counseling in Massachusetts?
Yes, all therapists at New Harbor Behavioral Healthcare are fully licensed to practice in Massachusetts. Our team includes Licensed Independent Clinical Social Workers (LICSWs), Licensed Mental Health Counselors (LMHCs), and psychologists who meet all state requirements to provide telehealth services to clients residing in Boston and throughout Massachusetts.
How much do telehealth counseling sessions cost, and do you accept insurance?
Our standard telehealth counseling sessions range from $120 to $175 per 50-minute session, depending on the provider’s credentials. We are in-network with several major Massachusetts insurance plans, including Blue Cross Blue Shield, Harvard Pilgrim, and Tufts. We also offer private pay options and can provide documentation for out-of-network reimbursement. Please contact us to confirm your specific insurance coverage.
What is the process to get started with telehealth counseling?
Getting started is simple. First, complete our online intake form or call our Boston office directly. Our intake coordinator will match you with a therapist based on your needs and preferred schedule. After your initial intake appointment, you’ll receive a secure telehealth link for your sessions. Most clients are able to schedule their first appointment within one week of contacting us.
How soon can I expect to see progress with telehealth counseling?
While every individual’s experience is unique, many clients begin to notice improvements in their mood and coping skills within 4 to 6 sessions. Your therapist will work with you to set specific goals and regularly review your progress. The timeline can vary depending on your concerns and the frequency of sessions, but we strive to help you achieve meaningful change as efficiently as possible.
Do you guarantee confidentiality and privacy for online sessions?
Absolutely. We use a secure, HIPAA-compliant telehealth platform to ensure your privacy and confidentiality. All session information is protected according to Massachusetts and federal laws. We recommend joining your sessions from a private, quiet space to further ensure your comfort and confidentiality.
